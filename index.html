Upcycle: The sweet spot 🌿

Upcycling takes something we already have and makes it more valuable, not less. That's different from recycling (which breaks it back down into raw material) and from disposal (which loses it forever).

Coffee grounds → a coaster you'll keep for 5 years. That's upcycling.

Coffee Scent Tags
咖啡香牌 · 祝福木牌
Shaped into blessing tokens with Chinese characters — 健康、幸福、平安 — and hung as fragrant ornaments.

How we made it

Coffee Scent Tags · 咖啡香牌
  1. 1
    Mix coffee grounds with wood-based binding powder (楠木粘粉) and water into a workable dough.
  2. 2
    Shape small rounded tags and press a string loop into the top before drying.
  3. 3
    Stamp or carve a blessing character — 幸福, 健康, 平安 — onto the face.
  4. 4
    Dry for 24 hours. Hang from backpack, door, or locker.
Tap to flip
Groups 3 & 4
Coffee Clay Creations
咖啡渣黏土作品
Functional everyday objects — coasters (杯垫), phone stands (手机支架), and small decorative items.

How we made it

Coffee Clay · 咖啡渣黏土
  1. 1
    Combine dried coffee grounds with modelling clay until the colour and texture feel right.
  2. 2
    Sketch the object — coaster, phone stand, tray — before shaping.
  3. 3
    Mould by hand or press into a flat template. Smooth the edges.
  4. 4
    Cure at room temperature for 1–2 days. Optional seal with clear varnish.

Coffee Body Scrub
咖啡磨砂膏
Used grounds + coconut oil + body wash.
A gentle exfoliant that smells like a café. The antioxidants in used grounds are actually good for skin. We packed it into small jars as gifts.
🕯️
Aromatherapy Candle
咖啡香熏蜡烛
Soy wax + grounds + cotton wick.
A thin layer of grounds suspended in wax releases a deep coffee-house scent as the candle burns. Best in small tins or mason-jar lids.

Coffee Soap
咖啡肥皂
Melt-and-pour glycerin soap + grounds.
A gentle scrubbing bar. The grounds sink slightly into the bottom of the mould, giving each bar a striped, layered look.
Our Journey · 04

How four phases unfolded. 📸

From a cardboard box of wet grounds to a classroom of finished products — here's the arc.

Phase 01 · Collecting

From the café counter.

Students visited the school café every afternoon to collect the day's used coffee grounds. Some days it was half a kilo. Some days it was nearly two.

01
Phase 02 · Drying

The patience phase.

We took the wet grounds home, spread them thin on trays, and dried them for two days. Wet grounds mould quickly — dry ones keep for weeks.

02
Phase 03 · Making

Hands-on crafting.

A three-hour session in the classroom: mixing, shaping, designing blessing characters, sculpting coasters. The whole room smelled like espresso.

03
Phase 04 · Presenting

Sharing what we learned.

Each group presented their interview data, their product, and the question they now wanted to keep exploring. Not a conclusion — a beginning.
